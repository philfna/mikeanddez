🤖 Getting Around SF
🤖
Try a Waymo — driverless robotaxi!
Waymo operates all over SF and it's one of the most uniquely SF experiences you can have. We love using it for Sunday's Yank Sing dim sum run — book Waymo One on your phone and a driverless Jaguar shows up. No driver. Just you and the car. Take a video. Everyone back home will want to see it.
🚃 The Powell-Hyde cable car runs right through the neighborhood — hop on at the top of Nob Hill and ride down to Fisherman's Wharf, or take it up from downtown. A classic SF experience steps from our front door.

What to Pack

SF in May is mild but tricky — the secret is layers. Here's what'll actually get used.

🧥 Clothes — the #1 thing visitors get wrong
SF May averages 55–68°F with cool foggy mornings and warmer afternoons. The temperature can swing 15°F in a single day, and it's always windier than you expect near the water.

💳 Practical Notes
Cash — SF is mostly cashless but a little cash is handy at the Ferry Building market vendors and some small shops.

Sunscreen — Even on foggy days, UV is surprisingly strong. The marine layer doesn't block UV the way clouds do.

Power bank — GPS, maps, and Waymo drain batteries fast on a full day out.

Comfortable shoes, seriously — Nob Hill is steep. Russian Hill is steep. Most of SF is hilly. Flip flops on day one is a classic mistake.

Dining time — We eat dinner around 5 PM, so you won't go hungry waiting for an 8 PM reservation like in other cities.
